Dataset: India's district-level literacy patterns using Census 2011

Key Findings:

  • Population distribution is highly uneven, with a few large states contributing a major share
  • Literacy varies widely (≈35–40% to >85%), with southern and western districts generally higher
  • Male and female literacy are strongly correlated, indicating parallel educational progress
  • Population size does not strongly predict literacy levels
  • Clear north–south geographic variation in literacy rates
  • States like Kerala and Tamil Nadu show consistently high literacy
  • Larger states exhibit wide internal variation in literacy rates
